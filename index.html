About Me

I am Kevin Cheng, currently a Masters student in Computer Science at USC. I graduated from UCSD with a B.S. in Bioengineering and started my career as a researcher. My introduction to programming started after I had already graduated and began working. It was during my first job that I truly came to appreciate how amazingly impactful, challenging, and awesomely fun developing and deploying software can be! I transitioned through a series of roles each one more software focused than the last. After working as a full-stack developer for a couple years, I realized that software development is the career that I want. I decided to go back to school to learn all the fundamentals of computer science to prepare myself for a long career.

Experience

A summary of my journey from research to engineering to software development

Research Associate

2012-2014

This is where I discovered how much of a force multiplier software can be. My role consisted of performing bench experiments and analysing data. I wrote my first scripts to automate some of the data analysis. This is also where I learned to program to robots to automate my own job.

Laboratory Automations Engineer

2014-2015

I worked on automating a liquid handling robot to isolate fetal DNA from a maternal blood sample, and prepare that sample for sequencing. The results informed parents of a panel of severe birth defects. Verinata NIPT

Software Engineer

2015-2018

As a software engineer at Illumina, the worlds leading genomics company. My main role was developing Local Run Manager, a web app that provides tools to setup DNA sequencing runs, perform bioinformatics analysis, and generate reports. I was also a maintainer for MiSeq Control Software, the on-instrument software for the most popular DNA sequencer. I am most proud of AmpliSeq for Illumina, which was at the time a highly secret collaboration with our largest competitor, where I served as the technical expert for the development of the analysis module.

Graduate Student

2018-present

I am a student at Univeristy of Southern California.
